Weapons

Ammo crafting makes another return albeit in a different form. Explosives are now used to create grenade launcher rounds, and enhanced ammo has been cut from the game.

  • Gunpowder combined with another of itself or creates handgun bullets.
  • Gunpowder combined with its high grade version creates shotgun shells.
  • RE2 remake's sub weapons can no longer be used as defense items. They must be used as if primary weapons.
  • High Grade Gunpowder combined with another of itself creates Magnum bullets.
  • Combining Explosive A with another of itself will give you explosive rounds.
  • Combining A with B gives you Flame rounds.
  • Combining B with itself gives you Acid rounds.

Knife

Once again, this will be your go-to melee weapon. Unlike RE2 Remake, it won't break. That means it's still great for knifing zombies.

G19

A 9mm Handgun that's obtained early in the game. It can be upgrade to improve focus and critical hit chance.

M3

A shotgun used by Jill. Like all shotguns before, it is best used on stronger enemies, headshotting zombies, or downing crowds of enemies. Upgrades include accuracy, fire rate, and reload rate.

Grenade Launcher

A versatile weapon used by Jill. Unlike the previous entries, ammo not only includes grenade, acid, and flame rounds. It also features mine rounds. The latter will have its use in one of the boss battles. So don't neglect them.

Lightning Hawk

A high grade magnum used by Jill and missable if you don't do some exploring. Like the previous entry, finding ammo is more scarce than shotgun ammo. However, thanks to ammo crafting, this is (sort of) mitigated. Again, use it on stronger enemies or bosses only.

G18 Burst

A handgun that can be obtained later in the game. It functions much like Leon's Matilda in RE2 and its remake.
